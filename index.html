Paneer Butter Masala

A rich and creamy North Indian curry with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a tomato, butter, and cashew-based gravy.

Ingredients

  • 200g Paneer, cubed
  • 2 large Onions, finely chopped
  • 3 Tomatoes, puréed
  • 1/2 cup Cashews, soaked
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste
  • Spices: Turmeric, Red Chili powder, Garam Masala
  • Fresh Cream for garnish

Preparation Method

  1. Heat butter in a pan, sauté onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add tomato purée and cook until the oil separates.
  4. Grind soaked cashews into a smooth paste and add to the gravy.
  5. Add spices, cook for a few minutes.
  6. Add paneer cubes and a little water. Simmer until gravy thickens.
  7. Garnish with fresh cream and serve hot with naan or rice.

Dal Makhani

A classic Punjabi dish made with whole black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma) slow-cooked with butter and cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Whole Urad Dal
  • 1/4 cup Rajma (Kidney Beans)
  • 1/2 cup Tomato Purée
  • 2 tbsp Butter/Ghee
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Cream
  • Spices: Cumin, Red Chili powder, Garam Masala

Preparation Method

  1. Soak dal and rajma overnight. Pressure cook until tender.
  2. In a separate pan, heat butter/ghee, add cumin.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, then tomato purée. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Add cooked dal and rajma, mix well.
  5. Add spices, simmer on low heat for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Stir in fresh cream just before serving.

Masoor Dal Tadka

A simple yet flavorful lentil curry made from red lentils, tempered with aromatic spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Masoor Dal (Red Lentils)
  • 1 Onion, chopped
  • 2 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 Garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 Green Chili, slit
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Preparation Method

  1. Wash and pressure cook masoor dal with turmeric and salt until tender.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil, add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add minced garlic and green chili, sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  6. Pour the cooked dal into the tempering mixture, mix well.
  7. Simmer for 5-7 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander.

Aloo Gobi

A popular dry Indian dish made with potatoes (aloo) and cauliflower (gobi) cooked with a blend of spices.

Ingredients

  • 2 Potatoes, diced
  • 1 small Cauliflower, florets
  • 1 Onion, chopped
  • 1 Tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic paste
  • Spices: Cumin seeds, Turmeric, Red Chili powder, Coriander powder, Garam Masala
  • Oil for cooking
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Preparation Method

  1. Blanch cauliflower florets for 5 minutes and drain.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped onion and sauté until golden.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste, then chopped tomato. Cook until soft.
  5. Add diced potatoes and cauliflower florets.
  6. Add all the dry spices, mix well.
  7. Cover and cook on low heat until potatoes and cauliflower are tender, stirring occasionally.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.

Chole Bhature

A popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (chole) and fried leavened bread (bhature).

Ingredients

  • For Chole: 1 cup Chickpeas (Chana), soaked overnight
  • 2 Onions, chopped
  • 3 Tomatoes, puréed
  • Ginger-Garlic paste
  • Chole Masala, Turmeric, Red Chili powder
  • For Bhature: All-purpose flour, Yogurt, Yeast, Sugar, Salt, Oil for frying

Preparation Method

  1. For Chole: Pressure cook soaked chickpeas. Sauté onions, ginger-garlic, and tomatoes. Add spices and cooked chickpeas, simmer.
  2. For Bhature: Knead a soft dough with flour, yogurt, yeast, sugar, salt, and water. Let it rise.
  3. Roll out small portions of dough into oval shapes.
  4. Deep fry bhature until puffed and golden brown.
  5. Serve hot chole with bhature, pickles, and sliced onions.

Vegetable Biryani

A fragrant and flavorful rice dish made with basmati rice, mixed vegetables, and aromatic spices, cooked in layers.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Basmati Rice
  • Mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes)
  • 1 Onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 Tomato, chopped
  • Ginger-Garlic paste
  • Biryani Masala, Turmeric, Red Chili powder
  • Yogurt, Mint leaves, Coriander leaves
  • Ghee/Oil

Preparation Method

  1. Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes, then cook 70% in salted water.
  2. Sauté sliced onions until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, then chopped tomatoes.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and spices, cook for a few minutes.
  4. Add yogurt, mint, and coriander leaves.
  5. Layer the cooked vegetables and partially cooked rice in a heavy-bottomed pot.
  6. Drizzle with ghee, cover, and cook on low heat (dum method) for 15-20 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with raita.
